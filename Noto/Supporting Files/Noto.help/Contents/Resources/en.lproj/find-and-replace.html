Find and replace text

Noto has search and replace text features to help you edit your document.

Open Noto for me

  1. Open the Find panel: Choose Edit > Find > Find

  2. Insert text to find: Type into the find box, or alternatively, select text anywhere on macOS and press ⌘E to insert it on the find field.

  3. Press “Next” to lookup and select the next occurrence the text. Alternatively, press ⌘G.

  4. Press “Previous” to lookup and select the previous occurrence the text. Alternatively, press ⌘⇧G.

Noto also allows you to replace occurrences of the desired text in your document.

  1. Open the Find panel: Choose Edit > Find > Find

  2. Insert text to find: Type into the find box, or alternatively, select text anywhere on macOS and press ⌘E to insert it on the find field.

  3. Insert text to replace: Type into the “Replace with:” box.

  4. Press “Next” to lookup and select the next occurrence the text. Alternatively, press ⌘G.

  5. Press “Replace” to replace the selected occurrence with the replacement text.

    Or

    Press “Replace & Find” to replace the selected occurrence with the replacement text and immediately lookup and select the next occurrence of the text to find.

  6. Press “Replace All” to lookup and replace all occurrences. Quickly replaces all the occurrences of the find text with the replacement text in one click.
